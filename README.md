# 📐 Determination of Young's Modulus by Flexure Method

![Experimental Setup](sddefault.jpg)
*Experimental Setup of Non-Uniform Bending Pin and Microscope*

---

## 🎯 Objective

To determine the **Young's Modulus of Elasticity** of the material of a given bar using the **flexure (bending) method**.

---

## 🧪 Theory

When a light bar of breadth **b** and depth **d** is placed horizontally on two knife-edges separated by a distance **L**, and a load of mass **m** is applied at the mid-point, the bar undergoes a depression **l**. The Young's Modulus **Y** of the material is given by:

$$Y = \frac{mgL^3}{4bd^3l}$$

Where:
| Symbol | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| `m` | Mass of the load (gm) |
| `g` | Acceleration due to gravity (cm/s²) |
| `L` | Distance between knife-edges (cm) |
| `b` | Breadth of the bar (cm) |
| `d` | Depth of the bar (cm) |
| `l` | Depression at mid-point (cm) |

> **Note:** This formula is valid as long as the slope of the bar at any point with respect to the unstrained position is much less than unity. In CGS units, Y is obtained in **dyne/cm²**.

---

## 🔬 Apparatus

- Spirit Level
- Steel Tape
- Screw Gauge
- Travelling Microscope
- Slide Callipers
- Metre Scale (used as the bar)
- Mass Hanger with Slotted Masses

---

## ⚙️ Experimental Setup

The setup consists of:
- A **beam (metre scale)** resting horizontally on two **knife edges**
- A **pin** placed at the mid-point of the beam
- A **mass hanger** with **slotted masses** hung at the centre
- A **travelling microscope** focused on the pin tip to measure depression

---

## 📋 Procedure

1. **Mark the bar** — Measure the length of the bar, mark its mid-point, and mark positions L₁L₁′ (e.g., 70 cm apart).

2. **Set up the knife-edges** — Mount the bar with its least dimension vertical on the knife-edges. Use a spirit level to ensure the bar is horizontal.

3. **Set up the microscope** — Position the travelling microscope so its axis is horizontal and the vertical scale is perfectly vertical. Focus on the pointer tip using the horizontal cross-wire. Eliminate parallax.

4. **Zero-load reading** — With no load on the hanger, record the microscope's vertical scale reading.

5. **Load the hanger** — Place a 0.5 kg load. Record the new microscope reading. The difference gives the depression for that load.

6. **Increase and decrease load** — Add loads in steps of 0.5 kg, recording readings at each step. Then remove loads in the same steps and record again. Take the mean of loading and unloading readings to get accurate depression values.

7. **Measure L** — Remove the bar carefully without disturbing the stands. Measure the distance between knife-edges accurately.

8. **Measure breadth b** — Using slide callipers, measure the breadth at three different positions. Calculate the mean. Apply zero-error correction if needed.

9. **Measure depth d** — Using a screw gauge, measure the depth at several positions along the bar. Calculate the mean. Apply zero-error correction if needed.

10. **Plot the graph** — Plot **load (m)** on the X-axis vs **depression (l)** on the Y-axis. The slope of the graph is used to calculate Y.

## ⚠️ Precautions

- Ensure the bar is horizontal before taking readings.
- Avoid parallax error while reading the microscope.
- Load and unload weights gently to prevent vibrations.
- Take readings at the same point each time.
- Apply zero-error corrections for screw gauge and callipers.
